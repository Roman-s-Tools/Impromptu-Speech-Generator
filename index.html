Official Rules for Impromptu Speeches

(Because panic should at least be organized.)

  1. Thou Shalt Speak on the Assigned Topic

    If the topic is “Why Pineapple Belongs on Pizza,” your speech should not somehow become a 4-minute TED Talk about your dog, your ex, or the Roman Empire.

    Creative interpretation is encouraged. Total abandonment is not.

  2. Respect the Time Limit

    You will be given a specific amount of time to speak.

    Going way too short makes it look like you gave up.

    Going way too long makes it look like we gave up.

    When the timer signals, land the plane.

  3. No Googling. No ChatGPT. No Carrier Pigeons.

    Impromptu means immediate.

    Once prep time ends:

    • No phones
    • No internet
    • No hidden scripts
    • No “I just happened to memorize this exact speech yesterday”

    Your brain is the main character here.

  4. Actually Answer the Prompt

    The audience should not spend the first minute wondering:

    “Wait… what was the topic again?”

    Make sure your speech connects clearly to the assigned subject, even if your logic takes a few scenic detours.

  5. Have a Beginning, Middle, and End

    A speech should not feel like a Netflix series canceled halfway through.

    Try this:

    Beginning: Introduce the idea

    Middle: Explain or defend it

    End: Finish confidently instead of whispering “...yeah.”

  6. Confidence Is More Important Than Perfection

    Nobody expects Shakespeare.

    We do expect you to pretend you know what you’re talking about.

    If you stumble:

    • Keep going
    • Smile
    • Say it louder like it was intentional

    This technique has worked for politicians for decades.

  7. Avoid Excessive Filler Words

    A few “ums” are normal.

    However, if your speech sounds like:

    “Um… like… so basically… um…”

    the audience may begin timing your filler words instead of your speech.

  8. Keep It Appropriate

    Do not:

    • Insult the audience
    • Start unnecessary drama
    • Reveal classified information
    • Begin conspiracy theories
    • Recreate a stand-up comedy special that gets everyone suspended

    Humor is encouraged. Chaos is not.

  9. Use Examples

    Stories, analogies, and dramatic exaggeration help.

    For example:

    “Trying to give an impromptu speech without a structure is like assembling IKEA furniture blindfolded while emotionally unstable.”

    That paints a picture.

  10. Make Eye Contact

    Look at the audience occasionally.

    Not:

    • Only at the ceiling
    • Only at the floor
    • Only at one deeply uncomfortable person in the front row

    Share the fear equally.

  11. Do Not Panic if You Forget Something

    Most audiences cannot tell the difference between:

    “A strategic pause”

    and

    “Complete mental collapse”

    Use this knowledge wisely.
